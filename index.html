About DHRS Medical Insistence System

     The health and medical care industry is an industry of great importance and intricacy. Health issues obviously relate to everyone, but because of the professional knowledge, complex execution and great amount of data involved, the health and medical care industry feels quite distant to non-professionals. For the same reason, people working in the industry, doctors for example, often faces great workload and pressure every day. And we believe there are machine-learning approaches simplify the process, reduce the amount human labor and make medical care more accessible to everybody.

     What we’re looking forward to establish is a tool which takes patients’ symptoms as inputs and gives basic diagnose and prescription as outputs. A tool like this can be used by both doctors and normal people. Doctors can use it to double check their diagnoses on patients or even help them making diagnoses, since the tool itself is an accumulation of knowledge and experience from the entire medical field. Normal people can use it to figure out what may be the explanation to some of the symptoms they’re having and whether it’s something they should be serious about and actually go to the hospital, or it’s not a big deal and they’ll heal themselves.

Input/Output

From 'symptems' to 'the most related medical department'

The raw input is a sentance or two describing the symptons of the paitient in words. For example,

"A 23-year-old white female presents with complaint of allergies."

The output we're expecting is the medical department this patient could most likely seek help from. In this case, the output should be

Allergy / Immunology

Word Vectorization

'Preprocess' and 'Bag of Words'

The first thing we need to do to preprocess the raw inputs. After steps such as removing punctuations, regularization, removing stop words, tokenization and lemmatization, a raw input such as "A 23-year-old white female presents with complaint of allergies." will be transformed into a list of words tokens are shown in the picture below.

Feature Extraction

Learning the 'key words' for every medical department

Our goal of feature (or key words) extaction is achieved through linear calssification. Performing a slightly twisted "one-verses-all" classification of all of the exixting department's sample set, we're alble to get hold of the top 15 weighted words for every class, AKA the most important 15 words for every medical departments, and their final weights after the training.
